I  ·  Introduction & Platform Overview

Overview

Providing pronunciation feedback to language learners has been shown to have a positive impact on their pronunciation skills (Kissling, 2013; Wang & Young, 2015), and technology-enhanced pronunciation feedback has been growing exponentially in recent years through the development of language learning applications and web-based platforms. Pronounce, founded by Olga Ayvazyan and introduced in July 2022, is one of these platforms. It is an Artificial Intelligence (AI) powered platform designed for language learners to practice English speaking skills and to analyze their output production to provide feedback on grammar, sentence correctness, lexical choice, and pronunciation, including mispronunciation and minimal pairs. Users can complete two primary tasks: a read-aloud task and an AI-powered conversation.

II  ·  Primary Features

The Read-Aloud Task

The first primary feature of Pronounce is the read-aloud task. Users record themselves reading a short text sample using the platform's speech record button. The platform offers one text suggestion daily. The text samples are written with a consistent reading level, medium to long sentences, relative clauses, moderate to high lexical diversity, and formal tone. Topics include health, physical activities, music, identity, social change, and multiculturalism. Users can also enter their own text to practice reading aloud. Figure 1 presents a text sample from the read-aloud task.

The AI-Powered Chat Function

The second primary feature of Pronounce is the chat function, in which users engage in an AI-powered conversation. Users are prompted to select a topic for the discussion or type the topic of their choice. Once the topic is selected, the AI partner begins the conversation with a broad question, generally directing the conversation toward the users' interests and preferences in relation to the topic. The conversation appears on the screen in an asynchronous format, and the users must click the answer button to respond in a spoken form. Users can see the written transcription and translation of the AI partner's responses and the transcription of their own responses. The hint button provides a response suggestion in a written form that users can use to support their oral response to the question of the AI partner. Users have to orally engage at least three times in the conversation to allow the platform to generate feedback. Figure 2 shows the features of the asynchronous AI-powered chat function.

III  ·  Critical Evaluation

Pronounce is a reliable, intuitive, and easy-to-use tool that language learners can use to practice and improve their speaking and pronunciation skills at their own pace without the social interaction pressure or the fear of mistakes.

— Evaluation Summary

Evaluating the Read-Aloud Task

The read-aloud task allows learners to practice speaking skills by reading a written text. A primary strength concerning this task is that learners can take as much time as needed to read the passage. The passage features high lexical richness, leading the users to practice with various common to less common vocabulary. However, the platform has some limitations as the topics are randomly assigned, and only one reading passage per day is available. Additionally, it does not offer the possibility of choosing a reading level, limiting the learner's opportunity to perform level-appropriate tasks.

Strength

Self-paced reading with no time pressure; high lexical richness supports vocabulary breadth.

Limitation

Randomly assigned topics; single daily passage; no option to select a reading level.

Evaluating the Chat Function

Another strength of Pronounce is the AI-generated conversation partner task, as it allows users to converse about any topic of their choice. The platform features an efficient AI-powered automatic speech recognition (ASR) system. Pronounce's ASR system is robust and highly efficient at recognizing and predicting language patterns, allowing even low-proficiency speakers to converse with the AI partner. Another strength of the chat function is the balanced and conciseness of the conversations. The asynchronous format allows users to anticipate and produce prepared responses, making this tool appropriate for beginners and intermediate-level learners. The platform is designed to engage the users meaningfully in the conversation to produce output. The AI responses are concise, often including questions to keep the conversation active. However, even though the conversation with the AI partner offers many advantages, its limitation resides in its ability to think critically and engage in critical discussions, as it mostly agrees with the statements of the users rather than evaluating whether or not the information provided is correct.

Strength

Robust ASR accommodates low-proficiency speakers; asynchronous format reduces anxiety and supports beginners.

Limitation

AI agrees uncritically with user input, limiting opportunities for factual evaluation or substantive debate.

The Feedback System

Research Background: Feedback in Pronunciation Instruction

The third primary feature of Pronounce is to provide feedback to the users. Explicit feedback leads to increased awareness about the production of these sounds. As previous research has suggested, providing feedback to the learners is critical (Kissling, 2013; Wang & Young, 2015). Kissling (2013) found that learners' production of the target phonemes had improved due to feedback and explicit instruction. Wang and Young (2015) found that providing feedback had a positive impact on the learners' speaking articulation and that it supported the learners' self-reflection and helped them track their speaking progress.

Pronounce delivers feedback in a separate window designed to encourage learners to engage with the material that requires improvement. Feedback is not provided during the task completion, allowing the users to remain focused on the tasks and avoid cognitive load. In the feedback section, a color system provides a comfortable visual for the users. Users can listen to the words individually by clicking on the words, allowing users to practice focused listening. Users can also listen to both the mispronounced vocabulary they recorded and the corrected version embedded in the sentence, enabling them to identify the context in which the word was used.

However, even though the feedback feature provides an efficient way to practice and a user-friendly interface, it presents some limitations, as the phonological transcription is available only for the corrected version. Additionally, the entire vocabulary word is indicated as mispronounced, providing no indication of which syllable contains the mistake. It does not provide information about which vowel or consonant needs practice, and there is no information on stress and intonation, leading the users to rely only on listening to identify the mistakes in the words labeled as mispronounced. Figure 3 shows the mispronunciation feedback for the word "reduction."

As language learners may be unable to distinguish between minimal pair sounds due to language transfer, feedback on minimal pairs is also provided, allowing the users to view the minimal pair adjacently to enable them to listen and compare their pronunciation with the AI speaker. Figure 4 shows the mispronunciation report for the minimal pair /i/ and /ɪ/.

After receiving feedback, the platform invites the users to practice the mispronounced words in isolation and embedded in three different sentences. By clicking on How to Improve, users receive explicit instructions on how each sound is pronounced. Users can click on the YouTube icon and receive explicit instructions about each sound of the target word, including phonological transcription. Additionally, common words with similar sounds are provided as examples. However, only three attempts can be made for each step, and other than the color system, symbolized by red and orange to indicate mispronunciation and green to indicate correct pronunciation, no individualized feedback is provided. Figure 5 shows the sample practice material provided by the platform for the word "reduction," which includes the word in isolation and in a sentence.

Limitations in Contextual Understanding

The feedback shows limitations in contextual understanding, as it may not always consider context. Figure 6 presents an erroneous feedback sample from a conversation with the AI partner, in which the topic discussed was grammar and the difference between "I should've gone" and "I should've went." In this particular example, the feedback recommended to the user to change "went" to "gone" even though it was not a grammatical mistake, but the topic of the conversation.

High Variability Phonetic Training (HVPT)

As the users can choose between four different varieties of English speakers, Pronounce can be helpful in high variability phonetic training (HVPT). Using multiple speakers and phonetic environments is an effective pronunciation technique that can result in improved pronunciation skills (Thomson, 2018). However, the platform currently supports only four different varieties of English (American, British, Australian, and Indian), limiting the opportunities for learners to have a conversation and be exposed to more varieties.

Using multiple speakers and phonetic environments is an effective pronunciation technique that can result in improved pronunciation skills.

— Thomson, 2018, on HVPT methodology

Accessibility & Subscription Model

Finally, Pronounce is accessible for free for users to try the platform. However, the free version contains many limitations, as users can only complete three 2-minute recordings. Once three recordings have been completed, users are directed to the subscription page, where they can subscribe and get access to unlimited recordings.

IV  ·  Conclusion

Pronounce is a user-friendly tool that language learners can use to enhance and improve their speaking and pronunciation skills by conversing with an AI-generated speaker about various topics. Overall, Pronounce can be an effective speaking and pronunciation practice tool that language learners can use, which language instructors could recommend for their learners. However, the platform's free version offers limited practice opportunities and feedback, and learners may not be willing to purchase the premium version. Additionally, there is a need for Pronounce to improve the AI model to improve authenticity and allow more profound factual information checks and critical discussions.

As of 2025, the designers have developed a mobile application named Speakerly AI, which consists of a mobile application version of Pronounce. Future reviews should consider investigating the mobile application version and evaluating its usefulness for language learning purposes.
